What do I want to do as VPHE?

Check out what I would like to do...

Transport I would like to improve the transport at our rural and island colleges so therefore we would have a better choice of bus times and for those who take evening classes they will be able to catch a bus which is at a time which suits them. We should also have a budget for mandatory residences such as the music students who have to pay their own way to their residences.

Accommodation Inverness too hot? Orkney non existant? Moray and LCC too expensive? and Perth too old? For a new student coming to the university you don't be wanting to hear that the cost of your flat being expensive before you even get a chance to pay for food or bills. Some students even have to do multiple jobs just to pay their rent. We need to get affordable accommodation for everyone all across the UHI.

Academic Representation To ensure that there's more options for students to represent their studies through their own academic partner. This could be organised by being able to get a voluntary certificate for being a Class Rep or other role such as President of a society. Or having voluntary officers at the majority of our partners for a range of different areas. e.g. liberation

Cross Campus Networks Recently in HISA's history we have created our very own LGBTQ+ Society but it should be the same for the rest of the liberation campaigns. So I would like to set out to create a Liberation Society for each and every liberation therefore a cross campus network. It will be a challenge but I love challenges so much! This would also improve the HISA attendance rate at Liberation conferences and make people more aware of what different liberations there are.  

Inclusion We should ensure that everyone feels welcomed and wants to get involved even if they have a different skin colour, different gender, they are disabled or even they represent LGBTQ+ everyone should feel included. We need to ensure that the Adventure Club events are inclusive to everyone with disabilities. We need to ensure that there are safe spaces where people can chat with others. 

Employability We should provide students, opportunities which can enhance their personal or professional skills, and to support them after they leave us here at UHI. Could be from stuff like joining one of our many societies to even doing a training course such as Health and Safety Training which even students can do, not just staff. We should also begin a mentor scheme which any new student across the UHI can opt-in to. Basically for a new student when you are starting your first day at the college it's daunting, sometimes you don't know anyone and all it takes is having this mentor there for those first few weeks and that could improve issues like having less early dropouts of courses.
